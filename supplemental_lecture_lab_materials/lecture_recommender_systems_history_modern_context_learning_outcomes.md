# Lecture: Introduction to recommender systems

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:

1. Describe major phases in recommender system evolution (from heuristic approaches to collaborative filtering and matrix factorization and ending with deep learning systems).
2. Explain how data scale, objective functions, and computational constraints shaped changes in recommender methods over time.
3. Explain the cold start problem and why it arises in recommender systems.
4. Distinguish between major types of recommender systems, including content-based, user-based collaborative filtering, item-based collaborative filtering.

---

## Instructor Notes

I personalize this lecture by connecting to events I remember and current issues I've read about.  For example,

- The Netflix Prize as a turning point in collaborative filtering and matrix factorization  
- Public controversy around TikTok’s recommendation algorithm and influence  
- KDD Cup 2024 and the emergence of LLM-based approaches  
- Regulatory attention (e.g., Amazon EU transparency case)  
- Companies attempting to influence how their products appear in LLM-generated recommendations  

These examples help students see recommender systems not just as technical tools, but as evolving socio-technical systems shaped by data, incentives, and external pressures.
