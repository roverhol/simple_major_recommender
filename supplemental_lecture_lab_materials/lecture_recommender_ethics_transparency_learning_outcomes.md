# Lecture: Ethical considerations in the design of recommender systems

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:


1. **Define fairness in recommender systems and explain why it is not a single objective**
   - Recognize that “fairness” can mean different things (for example, fairness to users vs. items).
   - Explain why fairness involves normative choices, not only technical ones.

2. **Identify different stakeholders affected by recommender systems**
   - Distinguish between users (for example, students choosing majors), providers (for example, departments or programs), and the platform or institution.
   - Explain how fairness concerns may differ or conflict across these groups.

3. **Describe common sources of bias in recommender systems**
   - Explain how historical data, popularity effects, and representation choices can lead to systematic disparities in recommendations.

4. **Explain tradeoffs between accuracy and fairness**
   - Recognize that optimizing for one metric may reinforce popularity or reduce diversity.
   - Articulate why fairness often requires modifying or balancing multiple evaluation criteria.

5. **Interpret and compare fairness-aware evaluation metrics**
   - Understand the purpose of metrics such as exposure fairness, group fairness, and diversity or coverage.
   - Explain what each metric captures and what it may overlook.

6. **Propose fairness-aware design choices for a recommender system**
   - Suggest concrete changes to data selection, similarity measures, clustering or ranking, or evaluation metrics.
   - Justify how these changes address a specific fairness concern.
